**Case Study: Phishing Attack on a College Student**

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. A common scenario involves college students receiving emails or messages that appear to be from their bank or university.

In this case, a student received an email claiming to be from their bank, stating that their account would be blocked unless they verified their details immediately. The email contained a link to a fake website that looked identical to the official bank website. Trusting the message, the student clicked the link and entered their login credentials and OTP.

Step-by-step, the attack worked as follows: first, the attacker sent a convincing email; second, the student clicked the malicious link; third, the student entered sensitive information; finally, the attacker used this information to access the bank account and transfer money.

Students and young adults are often targeted because they may lack awareness about such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and potential misuse of personal data.

This case highlights the importance of verifying sources, avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing confidential information online.
